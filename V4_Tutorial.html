🔑 What's New in V4 — Why Member Login Now Works

❌

Old method (V1–V3) — kept failing

App tried to match member by email string. This failed because emails in Firebase Auth and constants.js never matched exactly due to case differences, spaces, or wrong IDs.

✅

New method (V4) — guaranteed to work

Admin adds each member's Firebase UID directly into their member record. When member logs in, app matches by UID — which is always unique and exact. No more mismatches.

🔒

Your existing data is 100% safe

All payments, expenses, extra donations stay in Firebase. V4 only changes how members are identified after login. Nothing is deleted.

FAQ — Common Questions
Quick answers
Q

Will uploading V4 delete my existing payments and expenses?

No. All data is in Firebase. Uploading new code to GitHub never touches Firebase. Your payments, expenses, extra donations, and member records are all safe.

Q

My members were added via the app (not constants.js). Does V4 still work?

Yes! V4 works with members added any way — through the app or constants.js. You just need to edit each member in the Members tab and add their Firebase UID. The existing member IDs and payment data stay exactly as they are.

Q

What is a Firebase UID exactly?

It is a unique code Firebase assigns to every user account. It looks like: abc123XYZdef456 — about 28 characters. It never changes. It is the most reliable way to identify a user.

Q

A member forgot their password. What to do?

Tell them to tap "Forgot password?" on the login page. They enter their email → Firebase sends a reset link → they set a new password. No admin action needed.

Q

How do I add a new member in V4?

Step 1 — Firebase Auth → Add user → enter their email + password
Step 2 — Copy their UID from Firebase Auth
Step 3 — In your app, Admin → Members → + Add → fill name, email, join date, and paste UID
Step 4 — Share their email and password via WhatsApp

Q

Can I still use the old constants.js member list?

In V4, the constants.js SEED_MEMBERS is only used if your Firebase members collection is completely empty (first ever launch). Since you already have members in Firebase from before, constants.js is not used for your existing data. Your Firebase member records are the source of truth in V4.
